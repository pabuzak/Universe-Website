Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the first successful mission to land humans on the Moon and safely back to Earth. In July 16 1969, Apollo 11 was launched in 'NASA's Kennedy Space Center'. The astronauts that were the crew in Apollo 11 was Neil Armstron, born in 1930 and died in 2013, Edwin Aldrin, born in 1930 and Michael Collins, born in 1930 and died in 2021. The Apollo 11 mission was a goal that was set by the american President, John F. Kennedy. He made this decisision in 1961, and wanted a crew to make it to the Moon and safely back to Earth before a decade. In July 20 1969, Apollo 11 landed on the Moon. The astronauts were on the moon for 21 hours and 36 minutes, gathering samples for scientists. Neil Armstrong is the first person to walk on the moon. When he put his left foot on the moon, he stated: "That's one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind.".

Opportunity Rover

The Opportunity Rover, was a Mars exploartion rover and landed on mars in 2004, and there are been no response from the rover since 2018 due to the extreme dust storms on Mars. The rover was launched from Earth in 2003, and it's mission was to search for signs of past life on Mars. The rover was only planned to explore Mars for 90 days, but lasted for many more years. While on Mars, the rover made, any discoveries like finding that in the past, the conditions on Mars was able to support microbial life (almost any micropscopic organism like bacteria, archaea etc.). ANother discovery the rover found is that in the past, an area on Mars stayed wet for a long time. The rover traveled 45.16 kilometers, recharging it's batteries by using solar power and hibernated when dust storms took place.

Voyager Program

Voyager 1 and 2 are twin spacecrafts that were made to explore the Universe, studing our outer solar system. This is called the 'Voyager program' and Voyager 1 launched at September 5th 1977 and Voyager 2 launched at Augest 20th 1977 and both are still is active today. Voyager 1's target was to flyby Jupiter and Saturn while Voyager 2's target was to flyby Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. Voyager 2 discovered a 13th moon of Jupiter, discovered 10 new moons and two new rings at Uranus and discovered five new moons, four new rings and the famous "Great Dark Spot" at Neptune. Voyager 1 discovered a new ring and two new moons at Jupiter and discovered five new moons and a new ring at Saturn.
