Earth

Our home planet and the only planet that has been discovered to have life on it due to it being the only planet with oxygen in its atmosphere. Earth spins on an axis that extends from the North Pole to the South Pole as it circles the sun. A rotation on Earth's axis takes 23.934 hours, and an orbit around the sun takes 365.26 days. Earth is the fifth largest planet in our solar system, with a radius of 6,371 kilometers. Earth has two moons, the first called 'Moon' and the second called '1998 QE2'.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun and also known as the Red Planet. The Red Planet is so named because of the rusted iron in the ground giving the planet a bright red colour. Because the atmosphere in Mars is very cold and thin, water is unlikely to be on the surface for an extended period of time, Mars is still in the goldilocks zone (habitable zone). A rotation on Mar's axis takes 1 day and 37 minutes, and an orbit around the sun takes 687 days. Mars has two moons, the first called 'Phobos' and the second called 'Deimos'. Mars has a radius of 3,389.5 kilometers.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the sun and is a gas giant (a planet made of mainly gases). Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system with a radius of 69,911 kilometers. Jupiter is famous for the 'Great Red Spot', which is a giant storm on the planet that has lasted for many hundread years. Jupiter atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium. A rotation on Jupiter's axis takes 10 hours, and an orbit around the sun takes 4,333 days. Jupiter has 79 moons, 53 of them named and the other 26 is waiting to be named.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the sun and is a terrestrial planet (a planet with a rocky surface like Mars and Earth). Venus is commonly known as Earth's twin, due to the similar size of both of them. Even though Venus is the second planet from the sun, it is the hottest planet due to the sulfuric acid in it's atmosphere that traps heat. Venus' surface is dry, volanic and high volcanic mountains. A rotation on Venus' axis takes 116 days and 18 hours due to the planet spinning slowly in the oppositve direction of Earth, and an orbit around the sun takes 224.7 days. Venus has no moons and has the radius of 6,051.8 kilometers.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is a gas giant. Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system with a radius of 58,232 kilometers. The planet is most commonly known due to it's unique ring surround the planet which is made up of ice and rock. Saturn's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, like Jupiter. A rotation on Saturn's axis takes 10 hours and 45 minutes, and an orbit around the sun takes 10,756 days. Saturn has 82 moons, 62 of them named and the other 9 is waiting to be named.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is an ice giant (a planet with an icy core and has overall contains more ice than gas). The planet has a radius of 25,362 kilometers and has 27 moons. Uranus is known to be the sidewards planet as it spins on it's side and rotates in the opposite of Earth, like Venus. It is unique for having rings, in total 13 rings, like Saturn. A rotation on Uranus' axis takes 17 hours, and an orbit around the sun takes 30,660 days.

Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet from the Sun and is a terrestrial planet. It has no moons and is the smallest planet in our solar system with a radius of 2,440 kilometers and only a tiny bit larger than our Moon. Even though, Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun, it is not the hotteset due due to the thin atmospher containing mostly oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium and potassium. A rotation on Mercury's axis takes 59 days, and an orbit around the sun takes 175.97 days.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth or most furthest planet from the Sun and is a ice giant, like Uranus. There has only been one voyager to ever visit Neptune due to how long it takes to go there (the distance from the Sun is 4.5 billion kilometers). Neptune is known for it's "Great Dark Spot" which was found by Voyager 2. It's atmosphere is mostly made of hydrogen and helium and a little bit of methane and because of methane, Neptune is a bright blue colour. A roation on Neptune's axis takes 16 hours, and an orbit around the sun takes 165 years. Neptune has 0 moons and 5 main rings.
